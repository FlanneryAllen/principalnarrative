# Hal Riney — Simple Emotional Truths

**Essence:** Say the thing everyone feels but nobody says. Simple. Human. True.

---

## When to use:

- **Layers:** vision, mission
- **Unit types:** belief, value
- **Context:** brand campaigns, founder letters, "why we exist"

---

## Characteristics the agent checks for:

### 1. Short sentences that land emotionally
- **Pattern:** Simple subject-verb-object. No subclauses.
- **Why:** Each sentence should be complete. Let it breathe. Don't connect thoughts that deserve their own space.

### 2. Nostalgia without sentimentality
- **Pattern:** Evoke memory, not manipulation
- **Why:** Riney taps into universal human experiences without being mawkish. It's the difference between "remember when" and "remember why."

### 3. The universal in the specific
- **Pattern:** Talk about ONE thing that means EVERYTHING
- **Why:** Don't say "productivity." Say "the moment you realize you can see the whole thing." That's universal through specificity.

---

## Violations the agent detects:

| Pattern | Severity | Why it matters |
|---------|----------|----------------|
| Compound sentences with 'and' | ⚠️ warning | Riney breaks thoughts into separate sentences. Each idea deserves its own moment. |
| Abstract nouns (productivity, efficiency, innovation) | 🚫 error | Riney uses concrete images, not concepts. "The sun" not "illumination." |
| Exclamation points | 🚫 error | Riney doesn't shout. He whispers truths. Urgency through simplicity, not punctuation. |

---

## Examples

### ✓ Good:

**"The best builders don't want to code less. They want to see more."**
- **Why it works:**
  - Simple truth nobody says out loud
  - No cleverness, just clarity
  - Human longing stated directly
  - Two sentences, not one compound

**"Your codebase is alive. Watch it breathe."**
- **Why it works:**
  - Concrete image (not "dynamic system visualization")
  - Emotional without being sappy
  - Invitation, not explanation

**"It's morning again in America."** *(Riney classic)*
- **Why it works:**
  - One image that means everything
  - Nostalgia for possibility
  - No explanation needed

---

### ✗ Bad:

**"We empower developers to achieve unprecedented productivity through our AI-powered platform."**
- **Why it fails:**
  - Abstract, corporate language
  - No emotional truth
  - Compound sentence
  - Uses forbidden terms ("empower," "unprecedented," "AI-powered")
  - Explains the benefit instead of stating the truth

**"Our innovative solution helps teams collaborate more effectively and streamline their workflow."**
- **Why it fails:**
  - Compound sentence with 'and'
  - Abstract nouns everywhere (solution, teams, workflow)
  - "Helps" is weak — state the truth directly
  - Nobody feels anything reading this

---

## The Riney Test:

Read your sentence out loud.

**If it sounds like:**
- Something a real person would say → ✓
- A slide deck → ✗

**If it makes you feel:**
- Recognition of a truth you already knew → ✓
- Like you're being sold to → ✗

**If you removed all adjectives:**
- The sentence still works → ✓
- The sentence falls apart → ✗
