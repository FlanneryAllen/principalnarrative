How Streamline Technologies Reduced Review Time by 73%

CS
Customer Success Team
September 25, 2024 · 7 min read

When Jennifer Martinez joined Streamline Technologies as VP of Engineering, she inherited a team with a serious bottleneck: code reviews were taking an average of 3 days to complete. Senior engineers were spending over 6 hours per week reviewing code, and junior developers were stuck waiting for feedback instead of learning and shipping.

Six months after implementing CodePilot, Streamline had transformed their development workflow. Here's their story.

About Streamline Technologies

  • Industry: B2B SaaS (Supply Chain)
  • Team Size: 45 engineers
  • Tech Stack: TypeScript, Python, PostgreSQL
  • PRs/Week: 150-200

The Challenge: Review Bottlenecks Everywhere

Before CodePilot, Streamline's review process was typical for a growing engineering organization—and typically painful:

  • Average PR cycle time: 72 hours
  • Senior engineers spent 6+ hours/week on reviews
  • Junior developers waited 1-2 days for first feedback
  • Inconsistent review quality across team members
  • Sprint velocity suffered as PRs stacked up
"Our senior engineers were becoming full-time reviewers. They'd context-switch a dozen times a day to unblock PRs, and their own feature work kept slipping."

— Jennifer Martinez, VP of Engineering

Jennifer tried several approaches: establishing review SLAs, rotating review duty, even hiring dedicated "code review" engineers. Nothing stuck. The fundamental problem remained: not enough reviewer hours for the volume of code being written.

The Solution: AI-Augmented Review

Jennifer's initial skepticism about AI code review tools was understandable. She'd tried a few before—most were glorified linters that created more noise than signal. But CodePilot's approach was different.

"What sold me was the explainability. My engineers could actually understand why CodePilot was making suggestions, and they could disagree with it intelligently. It wasn't a black box."

The rollout happened in three phases:

1

Week 1-2: Pilot with one team

Started with the platform team (8 engineers). Ran CodePilot alongside existing review process to validate quality and build trust.

2

Week 3-4: Expand and configure

Rolled out to all teams. Configured Team Rules to match Streamline's coding standards and architectural patterns.

3

Month 2+: Full integration

CodePilot became the first pass on all PRs. Human reviewers focused on architecture, business logic, and mentorship.

What Changed

For Senior Engineers

Senior developers went from spending 6+ hours per week on reviews to spending 2 hours on high-value review work. The routine catches—style issues, common bugs, security patterns—were handled by CodePilot. Humans focused on:

  • Architecture decisions
  • Complex business logic
  • Mentoring junior developers
  • Knowledge transfer conversations

For Junior Developers

The most surprising benefit was for junior developers. Instead of waiting days for feedback, they got instant first-pass review within a minute. And because CodePilot explains its suggestions, every review became a learning opportunity.

"Our junior devs are learning faster than ever. Every PR teaches them something, and they're not waiting 2 days to find out they made a mistake."

For the Business

Sprint velocity increased by 31% as PRs moved through the pipeline faster. The engineering team shipped their Q3 roadmap a month early—the first time they'd beaten a deadline in two years.

Key Learnings

Jennifer shared several insights from Streamline's CodePilot implementation:

  • Start with trust-building. Run AI review alongside human review for the first few weeks. Let engineers see the quality before relying on it.
  • Configure Team Rules early. The faster you encode your team's standards, the more relevant suggestions become.
  • Redefine the reviewer role. Human reviewers should focus on what humans do best: context, architecture, mentorship. Let AI handle the routine.
  • Measure what matters. Track cycle time, not just review count. The goal is shipping faster, not reviewing more.
