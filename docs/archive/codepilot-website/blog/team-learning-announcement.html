Introducing Team Learning: AI That Adapts to Your Codebase

AS
Alex Santos
Product Lead · November 15, 2024 · 5 min read

Every engineering team is different. You have your own conventions, your own architectural patterns, your own hard-won lessons about what works in your codebase. Generic AI tools ignore all of this, applying one-size-fits-all rules that often miss the mark.

Today, we're launching Team Learning—a new CodePilot feature that learns your team's unique patterns, preferences, and best practices over time. The more you use CodePilot, the smarter it gets about your codebase.

What Team Learning Does

🎯

Learns from Feedback

When you accept or dismiss suggestions, CodePilot adjusts its understanding of your preferences

📚

Captures Team Patterns

Recognizes coding patterns that are common in your codebase and suggests consistency

🧠

Preserves Knowledge

Institutional knowledge from senior developers gets encoded and shared with the whole team

📈

Improves Over Time

Suggestion relevance increases as CodePilot learns more about your codebase

How It Works

Team Learning operates on a simple feedback loop:

1. Observe

CodePilot watches how your team writes code and responds to suggestions. It notes patterns like:

  • Error handling conventions you consistently use
  • Naming patterns in your codebase
  • Architectural patterns specific to your system
  • Which suggestions you accept vs. dismiss

2. Learn

From these observations, CodePilot builds a model of your team's preferences. This isn't just counting—it understands why patterns matter in context.

3. Apply

Future suggestions are informed by this learned context. CodePilot doesn't just flag "this could be different"—it flags "this doesn't match how your team typically handles this situation."

4. Refine

As you continue to accept and dismiss suggestions, the model refines. Team Learning is never "done"—it adapts as your codebase and practices evolve.

Real Example: Error Handling

Let's say your team has a convention: all database queries should be wrapped in a specific error handler that logs to your observability platform and includes request correlation IDs.

Generic AI tools don't know this. They might suggest adding try/catch blocks, but not your specific pattern.

With Team Learning, CodePilot notices that:

  • 97% of database calls in your codebase use withDbErrorHandler()
  • The 3% that don't were flagged in previous reviews
  • Your team consistently accepts suggestions to add this wrapper

Now, when a new PR has a raw database call, CodePilot specifically suggests adding withDbErrorHandler()—not just generic error handling.

Before Team Learning

"Consider adding error handling to this database query."

After Team Learning

"This database query should be wrapped in withDbErrorHandler() for consistency with your team's error handling patterns. 97% of similar queries in this codebase use this wrapper."

Privacy and Control

Team Learning is designed with privacy in mind:

  • Team-scoped: Learning is specific to your organization. Your patterns are never shared with other customers.
  • Pattern-level only: We learn patterns and preferences, not your actual code. No source code is stored for learning.
  • You're in control: View what CodePilot has learned in your Team Settings. Reset or adjust at any time.
  • Self-hosted compatible: For enterprise customers, Team Learning works entirely within your infrastructure.

Getting Started

Team Learning is now available for all Team and Enterprise plans. Here's how to get the most from it:

  1. Keep reviewing: Team Learning improves with use. The more PRs your team reviews with CodePilot, the better it gets.
  2. Be consistent with feedback: Accept suggestions you like, dismiss ones you don't. This feedback trains the model.
  3. Check your Team Patterns: Visit Settings → Team Learning to see what patterns CodePilot has identified.
  4. Combine with Team Rules: For explicit standards, use Team Rules. Team Learning handles the implicit patterns.
